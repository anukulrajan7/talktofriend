# Privacy Policy

**TalkToFriend** — last updated: April 2026

---

Brutally honest privacy policy. No legalese.

---

## What data exists

### Data that touches the server

| Data | Where stored | How long |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Room codes | SQLite | 24 hours, then deleted |
| Chat messages | SQLite | 24 hours, then deleted |
| Who's in a room | Memory only | Until room ends |

That's the complete list. Nothing else.

### Data that never touches the server

- Your video and audio (P2P mesh mode: goes directly browser-to-browser)
- Your real name (you never gave one — it's optional and unverified)
- Your IP address (not logged)
- Any persistent identifier

## SFU mode (server-assisted calls)

When a call uses the SFU (Selective Forwarding Unit) path, media passes through the server to improve quality for larger groups. This media is:

- Forwarded in real-time to other participants
- Never recorded
- Never stored
- Gone the moment the call ends

## What we do not collect

- No accounts, emails, or passwords
- No cookies
- No analytics (no Google Analytics, no Plausible, no Mixpanel, nothing)
- No error tracking (no Sentry, no Datadog, nothing)
- No advertising IDs
- No device fingerprinting
- No cross-session tracking of any kind

## Third-party services

None. The app makes no requests to third-party services during a call. No external fonts loaded at runtime, no CDN telemetry, nothing phoning home.

## Self-hosted instances

If someone else is running TalkToFriend on their own server, that operator controls everything — their server, their data, their rules. This privacy policy only applies to instances operated by the original author.

When self-hosting, check what version they're running and whether they've modified anything.

## Your rights

Since we don't know who you are and don't store anything about you beyond 24 hours, there's nothing to delete or export. Your data is already gone.

## Questions

Open an issue on GitHub. That's the support channel.
